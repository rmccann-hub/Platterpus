## A. What was systemically wrong (the patterns, not the instances)

1. **Two events outran the docs.** Almost every high-severity finding traces to
   (a) the **whipper removal** (2026-06-30, KDD-18) — test-plan steps, TASKS
   items, PLANNING sections, and READMEs still directed readers at commands,
   Settings rows, and proofs that stopped existing — and (b) the **CTDB CRC
   hardware-validation** (2026-07-07, v0.4.20) — the v0.4.23 caveat sweep
   cleaned the *app copy* but missed ~10 doc locations still saying
   "experimental / blocked / pending".
2. **Duplicated facts drifted, single-homed facts didn't.** Every triple-tracked
   status (CTDB verify in three TASKS sections, the UX gap table in two homes,
   the EAC-parity story told twice in README, the issue bodies pasted in two
   places) had drifted; facts with one canonical home stayed correct. This
   validates the SSOT rule — the fixes therefore *declared canonical homes*
   (TASKS current-plan item 8 for CTDB status; `scripts/cyanrip/` for paste
   text) rather than just re-syncing copies.
3. **The CI/release reality of 2026-07-08 was recorded nowhere central.** Seven
   gating/advisory CI jobs, weekly mutation runs, `.zsync` + provenance release
   assets — shipped in the trust-audit hardening but absent from CLAUDE.md,
   testing.md, and DEPENDENCIES.md until now.
4. **The CHANGELOG had lost a release.** The `v0.3.10` tag exists on GitHub but
   its section had been absorbed into `[0.4.0]` — recovered verbatim from the
   tag. Conversely `v0.2.0`/`v0.0.1` links pointed at tags that don't exist.
5. **Doc-count is not the problem; framing is.** Most "redundant" docs are
   dated *records* (investigations, audits) that only needed banners saying so.
   The genuinely consolidatable clusters are small (see the map).
